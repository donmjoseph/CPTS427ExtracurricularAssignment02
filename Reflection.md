**Completed:** April 4, 2026

---

## Activity Description

The activity I scouted for EA-2 was IBM's Cybersecurity Case Studies and Capstone Project, a 16-hour course offered through Coursera. My career goals span Technical Project Management, IT Consulting, and Cybersecurity — this course aligned with all three by developing practical skills in breach analysis, incident response evaluation, and organizational risk assessment.

The course was structured across six modules, each built around analyzing real-world cybersecurity incidents. Topics included phishing and vishing scams, point-of-sale breaches, insider threats, AI-related data breaches, ransomware attacks, incident response, digital forensics, and penetration testing case studies. The expectation was to analyze each case study through a structured template and ultimately complete a capstone project applying the same methodology to a self-selected, real-world data breach not covered in the course.

---

## Technical Decisions

The most significant technical decision I made was selecting the 2023 MGM Resorts International cyberattack as my capstone case study. I chose this breach because it demonstrated a multi-layered attack chain — social engineering at the help desk, privilege escalation through Okta and Azure, lateral movement, and ransomware deployment across 100+ ESXi hypervisors. It touched on nearly every domain covered in the course: vishing, incident response, digital forensics, and compliance failures.

Structurally, I chose to document the analysis in Markdown rather than a word processor to keep the deliverable version-controlled and repo-friendly. I also relied on primary and verifiable secondary sources — SecurityWeek and BleepingComputer — rather than aggregator blogs, to ensure citation credibility. Throughout the analysis I applied the NIST Incident Response framework implicitly when evaluating MGM's containment and recovery actions, connecting academic concepts to a real operational failure.

## Contributions

I participated in this activity individually. All course modules, graded assessments, and the capstone project were completed independently. The breach analysis, citation research, and structured writeup in the capstone are entirely my own work.

## Quality Assessment

I'd rate my participation as strong overall. Finishing the course in a compressed timeframe while maintaining a 93.40% grade reflects consistent effort across all modules. The capstone deliverable is thorough — it covers all eight required sections with specific, well-sourced observations rather than generic statements.

If I could redo this, I would have started earlier and spent more time on the penetration testing and digital forensics modules specifically, as those are the areas most directly applicable to a cybersecurity career. I also would have supplemented the course with hands-on labs — TryHackMe or a similar platform — to pair the analytical skills developed here with practical technical experience. Case study analysis builds strong conceptual understanding, but cybersecurity ultimately requires tool-level proficiency that this course format doesn't fully address. That's the clearest gap I identified and a concrete next step for future extracurricular work.
